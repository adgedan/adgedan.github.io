Are you compliant

As a company, COCO Lighting has a policy of continual improvement. As such it has recently invested heavily in the latest Seaward HAL testing and EMC equipment. This investment, along with its internal operations, practices and quality control systems are designed to define the marking, testing and responsibility requirements for re-engineering luminaires into emergency lighting complying, where applicable, with the requirements of:

EN 60598-1, EN 60598-2-22, EN 61347-1, EN 61347-2-3 Annex J, EN 61347-2-7,EN 61347-2-13, EN 55015, EN 61547 and EN 61000-3-2.

These standards cover both fluorescent and LED light sources and specifies that the entire process should be carried out under the control of a competent person in an ISO 9001:2008 registered quality control environment.

What are the lighting safety requirements

All of the products that we supply meet the relevant British Safety requirements. These include BS EN 60598-1 (Luminaires part 1: General requirements and tests), BS EN 61347-2-7 (emergency lighting product standards), and BS EN 60598.2.22 (Luminaires for emergency lighting, incorporating all advances in regulations).
