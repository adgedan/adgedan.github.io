INTELLIGENT LIGHTING CONTROLS SAVES ENERGY
AND IMPROVES COMFORT

Current building control regulations require buildings to be built or refurbished to the highest of energy saving standards.

A Lighting Control System can help eliminate energy waste and improve comfort by balancing the light provided by the building and the light needed by occupants. For example a restaurant may use scene setting to change the ambience from brighter at lunchtime to softer and darker in the evening.

LIGHTING CONTROL TECHNIQUES

Automated lighting control systems can be a great tool for helping you to save money for your businesses. It can help you to turn off lights and power where it isn't required or has been left running.

THE BENEFITS LIVE DEMO

SENSORS

Sensors used in high traffic areas such as hallways, corridoors, toilets, storage areas and external entries can be a great power saving addition for your business lighting control system. It helps to avoid a common scenario where an occupant switches on the lights in these areas and forgets to turn them off, or only needs the light in the hallway say for a few moments. The sensor will detect movement in the area and leave the lights on while the occupant remains, and turn off after a set amount of time.

ON/OFF BUTTONS

"All Off" buttons are a quick easy way for the occupant to turn off lighting or power in whole areas. You may have this button at your reception to switch all office lights when you leave at night. Or in the main office for when you leave for the day. The whole business can be switched off with a single press of a switch.

You don't necessarily need to switch of the entire business lighting. Recently we programmed a button to switch off a section of a lights for a business. This gave the client the ability to turn off all the office, meeting room but leave just reception lighting on.

TIMERS

Timers can be used for power saving techniques as well. A timer can switch off all external light when the sun rises, or late at night. You can also build timers into buttons, so a light or heat button can have a built in timer to turn off after a given length of time. Energy lost by heated towel rails left on by kids was an issue we tackled recently in a home at Hope Island, Gold Coast. No matter if the user was to turn the heated towels on and leave them on, our programming would switch them off within a couple of hours.

REMOTLEY

When you know you've left power on or you just like to be sure, log into your system with your iPad or PC and switch off individual lights or power, or even the whole business. That's great peace of mind and helps save energy usage.

Benefits of lighting controls

Large electricity savings

Some copy to go here on the quick wins on energy saving by installing controls into your business.

 “Lighting uses around 20% of the electricity generated in the UK; non-domestic lighting is responsible for around 24 million tonnes CO2 / year.”

The Carbon Trust

Safety and security

Movement sensors can be installed to detect presence and/or absence so lights are automatically turned off when a room is unoccupied enabling you to save energy. From sensors installed lights can automatically turned on to pre-programmed levels when an occupancy is detected improving safety and also security.

Ambience improvements

Lighting plays such an important role in the functionality and enjoyment of a space and LEDs are a big winner here to. Now coming in a range of different colour temperatures LEDs are able to produce a greater sense of daylight indoors. This is healthier and more appealing to the human eye, especially where visual impairment may be an issue.

The impact of the LED lighting on colour temperature is also clear. The cooler, brighter light has greatly enhanced the ambience within most of the sites. Colour rendering has also improved, so that colours appear much sharper.

The Energy Saving Trust
